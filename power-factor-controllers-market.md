<p><p>Power Factor Controllers (PFCs) are critical in optimizing energy efficiency in electrical systems. The market for PFCs includes two main types: Active Power Factor Controllers and Passive Power Factor Controllers. Active PFCs use advanced electronic circuitry to dynamically adjust power factor, improving efficiency and reducing harmonic distortion. Conversely, Passive PFCs rely on inductors and capacitors to correct power factor but are less efficient and adaptable. Both types play vital roles in reducing energy consumption and enhancing system performance in industrial and commercial applications.</p></p>
