<p><p>The 5G transceiver market features two primary types: single-chip design and standalone chip design. Single-chip design integrates multiple functions into one chip, enhancing compactness and efficiency, making it ideal for cost-sensitive applications. In contrast, standalone chip design separates the transmitter and receiver functions into distinct chips, allowing for greater flexibility and performance tuning. This design is preferable for high-performance applications, where customization and specialized features are crucial. Both types cater to diverse market needs, balancing cost, size, and performance.</p></p>
